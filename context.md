# POPS website context.md

## What this file is
This is the master context for building the POPS website with AI. It combines:
- site strategy and structure
- page-by-page guidance
- merch copy
- reusable website copy
- brand and visual direction
- implementation notes for making the site feel very close in structure and rhythm to Democrats.org while still being distinctly POPS

---

## Core assumptions
A few assumptions are baked into this draft because the full final platform text was not included in this thread. The copy below treats POPS as:
- a modern, youth-driven American political party or movement
- strongly pro-democracy, pro-rights, pro-opportunity, and anti-corruption
- focused on practical reform over empty rhetoric
- serious, patriotic, contemporary, and digitally native

Where the exact platform wording matters, the site should pull from the final approved POPS platform document and swap in the precise issue language.

---

## Brand summary
POPS should feel:
- patriotic without looking old or stale
- bold, clean, and modern
- serious enough for politics, sharp enough for Gen Z and young voters
- optimistic, but not corny
- clear, direct, reform-minded, and slightly insurgent in tone

The existing logo already does a lot of the work. The flag treatment plus the heavy black wordmark gives the brand a strong campaign aesthetic. The merch mockups suggest the identity works best when used simply, with plenty of white space.

---

## Overall website goal
The site should do four things well:
1. introduce POPS fast
2. explain the platform in a skimmable but serious way
3. convert visitors into supporters, volunteers, subscribers, and donors
4. make the movement feel real, organized, and already in motion

The site should not feel like a generic student project page. It should feel like an actual national political site prototype.

---

## Reference direction: Democrats.org format
The site should be very similar to Democrats.org in structure, pacing, and interaction model, meaning:
- clean campaign-style homepage with large blocks and strong calls to action
- simple top navigation
- clear donation / join / volunteer buttons
- issue-based sections written for real voters, not policy wonks only
- a news / updates area
- an action-oriented footer
- highly legible typography
- strong use of white, dark text, and red/blue accent

Do **not** copy their exact design one-to-one. Instead, mirror the format principles:
- bold hero
- fast explanation of mission
- action modules
- issues preview
- merchandise / shop preview
- updates / organizing / calls to action
- substantial footer

## Platform page writing guidance
The platform page should sound:
- clear
- grounded
- morally confident
- specific without sounding bureaucratic
- serious enough for adults, readable enough for students

Each issue section should use this format:
1. issue title
2. 1-2 sentence principle statement
3. short bullet list or paragraph on what POPS would do
4. optional deeper explainer or linked white paper

### Example issue-page intros

#### Democracy and anti-corruption
Democracy cannot survive if public power is quietly sold to private interests. POPS supports aggressive anti-corruption reforms, stronger ethics rules, transparency, voting access, institutional accountability, and a politics that answers to citizens rather than donors, cronies, or entrenched machines.

#### Healthcare and public health
Healthcare should be judged by whether it keeps people alive, healthy, and treated with dignity - not by whether a broken system can continue extracting from them. POPS supports a more accessible, evidence-based, humane healthcare system that treats public health as a national obligation rather than an afterthought.

#### Opportunity and affordability
A country cannot claim to believe in opportunity while basic stability becomes unaffordable. POPS supports policies that make it easier to build a life: decent wages, affordable essentials, upward mobility, and an economy that rewards work more than proximity to power.

#### Education and youth opportunity
Education should open doors, not reproduce inequality with better branding. POPS supports strong public education, civic literacy, real pathways into work and higher learning, and a politics that takes the aspirations of young people seriously.

#### Rights and dignity
A free country does not selectively apply dignity. POPS supports equal protection, civil rights, bodily autonomy, and the principle that law and policy should expand liberty and human security rather than narrow them.

## AI build guidance
Use this section as direct context for an AI website builder or coding model.

### High-level product brief
Build a political campaign-style website for POPS. The site should feel structurally similar to Democrats.org: clean, bold, credible, action-oriented, modern, and highly legible. The design should use strong white space, large headings, clear navigation, clear calls to action, and a patriotic but contemporary visual system.

### Visual direction
- primary background: white or near-white
- text: black or very dark charcoal
- accents: strong red and deep blue taken from the logo
- overall feel: premium campaign brand, youthful but serious
- avoid gradients unless extremely subtle
- avoid glassmorphism, neon, excessive shadow, or startup-app aesthetics
- typography should feel editorial, modern, and political
- use generous spacing and confident scale

### Layout direction
- large homepage hero
- section-based scroll experience
- repeated CTA bands between sections
- image-supported merch grid
- issue cards or anchored issue sections
- newsletter signup modules
- large footer with action links

### Tone direction
- assertive
- hopeful but not cheesy
- patriotic without cliche overload
- intellectually serious but readable
- reform-minded
- anti-cynicism
- clean and persuasive

### Content rules
- write like a real movement, not a parody of one
- avoid vague platitudes when possible
- keep paragraphs relatively short
- prefer strong declarative sentences
- every major page should include at least one action prompt
- the site should communicate momentum, discipline, and legitimacy

### Homepage must include
- hero with headline, body copy, and 2-3 CTAs
- mission statement section
- platform preview cards
- why now section
- action strip
- news preview cards
- merch preview using supplied mockups
- footer signup and donate CTA

### Platform page must include
- issue navigation or anchor links
- major issue sections
- plain-English summaries
- optional expandable detail areas
- final CTA to join, donate, or organize

### Merch page must include
- hero section
- product grid using provided mockups
- product titles and descriptions from this file
- all items visibly marked out of stock
- optional restock notification form

### Footer should include
- About
- Platform
- Organize
- News
- Merch
- Donate
- Join Us
- social links placeholders
- legal / contact / privacy placeholders

## What to avoid
- do not make it look like a meme campaign
- do not overload it with stars, flags, or patriotic cliches beyond the logo system
- do not use cluttered policy walls on the homepage
- do not use overly academic issue-page language

- do not make the tone sound sarcastic, self-aware, or unserious
